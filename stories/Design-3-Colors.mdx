<Title>Colors</Title>

I wanted to work with &ldquo;warm&rdquo; colors, but... not a designer, selecting and running with colors is a weak point of mine. I used to use Adobe Kuler, it is now Adobe Color and is just not the same. I wound up with palettes from Adobe Color, [Happy Hues]("https://www.happyhues.co/palettes/11"), and [Coolors]("https://coolors.co/visualizer/606c38-fefae0-283618-dda15e-bc6c25").

I needed more contrast and variation than these offered, but I still use colors from these to start with and adjusted as needed.

## Tokens

My [color] token naming scheme tries to follow [https://tr.designtokens.org/format/#design-token](https://tr.designtokens.org/format/#design-token).

It&rsquo;s something like:<br />
property[->for[->for?]?]: value<br />
e.g.<br />
`color-text-primary: #000;`

## Background gradient

Can we not talk about this? At the onset of this project there was a whole design concept I was trying to go for, think like Material UI but with... liquid. It proved too difficult and time-consuming for me to get into, and this gradient I was playing with stuck. Given its assignment on the `<body>`, its aesthetic changes based on the height of the document. It gives shorter pages a different feel from more content-heavy pages. Does it go with the angle of light, shadow, depth, etc.? Does it _need_ to? I can sleep at night.

I've broken down the gradient to its 3 colors, especially for a11y purposes, spread between top of the page, middle, and bottom:

## highlight.js syntax highlighting theme

Over the years I have tried different editors: Notepad, Notepad++, TextPad (I still use this), BB Edit, Coda 2, Atom, Brackets, and now VS Code. My favorite theme that stuck with me was Coda 2&rsquo;s, so every environment I endeavor to replicate it where possible. I picked the bright [Github theme]("https://github.com/highlightjs/highlight.js/blob/main/src/styles/github.css") because it&rsquo;s the closest theme I could find. Maybe one day I&rsquo;ll roll my Coda 2 syntax highlighting theme. Not sure why, but I can&rsquo;t get used to dark editor themes.
